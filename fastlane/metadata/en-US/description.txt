Press Cmd+C twice in any app and a small toast pops up with the translation, right where you're working. You never have to switch apps or paste anything into a box.

CCTrans lives in your menu bar and stays out of the way until you need it.

HOW IT WORKS
• Copy any text twice (Cmd+C, Cmd+C) and the translation toast appears
• Press Shift+Cmd+2 to capture the screen and translate whatever text is on it
• The menu bar icon holds the rest: languages, toast position, request logs

PRIVATE BY DEFAULT
The default translator is Apple's on-device engine, so the text you copy never leaves your Mac. CCTrans ships without analytics or accounts, and the developer runs no servers. There is nothing to collect and nowhere to send it.

BRING YOUR OWN AI (OPTIONAL)
If you want LLM-quality translations instead, paste in your own OpenRouter API key. CCTrans can also attach a capture of your screen as context, which makes a real difference for short snippets that could mean three different things on their own.

MADE FOR REAL WORK
• Works in every app: browsers, editors, chat, PDFs
• The toast shows up where you put it and goes away on its own
• Language pairs download once, then translation works offline (Apple Translation)
• A plain menu bar app with no Dock icon to get in your way

PERMISSIONS
• Input Monitoring (optional) lets CCTrans notice the Cmd+C double-press in other apps. Without it, copying the same text twice still triggers the translation through the clipboard.
• Screen Recording (optional) is used for screenshot translation and the screen-context capture, nothing else.

CCTrans is free and open source.
